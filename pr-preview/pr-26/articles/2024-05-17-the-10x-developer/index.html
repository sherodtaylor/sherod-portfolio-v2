The 10x Developer

2024-05-17

A dev, a rockstar, a 10x-er. Speed vs efficiency? Could it be knowledge or just general ability?

I think what separates the great from the average is their ability to work ahead or be curious. A lot of engineers get complacent or see the average workload from their team and decide that’s the level we should operate on. The lack of curiosity or imagination on what we can improve is what stops good engineers. The most educated person is the one who seeks knowledge and challenges the status quo — in their job or in life.

How to stay curious

With our jobs we become bored. We work on the same project that comes in different iterations that only change ever so slightly. Then we continue with the same requirements and don’t try to push the envelope. What people are missing is what interests them. We don’t question our managers enough or give them ideas.

Proofs of Concept

Most engineers don’t put on their thinking caps to brainstorm what could be. Think about what could be or any crazy thoughts you have. Then create a POC — a way to learn or become interested in the work you do. As you create the POCs, push your managers to show them a better way. More than likely they will love the effort you put in and how it benefits the team.

In my career the best engineers I know stay curious and challenge the status quo. They see a problem and fix it on the spot or propose a better way. Don’t get complacent — keep what you do interesting. Not all ideas will be accepted, but the ones that do have the ability to make a big impact.

On my current team the last four major initiatives started out as POCs I built in the margin. Over the past four years we’ve created a team around the products I designed and pushed. Stay curious and build POCs — it’s the most fun and validating way to have a major impact.
